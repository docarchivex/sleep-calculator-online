3. Sleep Calculation Accuracy

Our sleep calculator uses the following assumptions:

  • 90-minute sleep cycle model: Based on widely accepted sleep research. However, individual sleep cycles can range from 70 to 120 minutes and vary from person to person.
  • 14-minute sleep latency: The average time to fall asleep. Your actual sleep latency may be significantly shorter or longer depending on factors like stress, caffeine intake, physical activity, and sleep environment.
  • Uniform cycle length: The calculator assumes all cycles are equal in duration. In reality, early-night cycles tend to have more deep sleep, while later cycles have more REM sleep.

Therefore, the calculated times are estimates only and should be used as general guidance, not precise medical prescriptions.

  • Travel and jet lag
  • Screen time and blue light exposure
